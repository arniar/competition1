About Dr. Tessy Thomas

Dr. Tessy Thomas is a distinguished Indian scientist and aerospace engineer known as the "Missile Woman of India." Born in 1963 in Alappuzha, Kerala, she has made groundbreaking contributions to India's missile development programs, particularly the Agni series of ballistic missiles. As the first woman to lead a missile project in India, Dr. Thomas has broken gender barriers in the field of defense technology and inspired countless young women to pursue careers in science and engineering.

With over three decades of experience at the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO), Dr. Thomas has been instrumental in developing various missile systems that have significantly enhanced India's defense capabilities. Her exceptional technical expertise, leadership skills, and unwavering dedication have earned her numerous accolades and recognition both nationally and internationally.

Beyond her technical achievements, Dr. Thomas is known for her humility, perseverance, and determination. She has consistently advocated for women's participation in science and technology, serving as a powerful role model for future generations. Her journey from a small town in Kerala to becoming one of India's most respected scientists exemplifies the power of education, hard work, and pursuing one's passion regardless of societal expectations.

Her Inspiring journey

1963 - 1980
Early Years & Education
Born in Alappuzha, Kerala, Tessy developed an early interest in mathematics and science. Named after Mother Teresa, she was encouraged by her father to pursue her academic interests. She completed her schooling from St. Michael's Higher Secondary School, excelling in mathematics and science subjects.
1980 - 1988

Higher Education

Tessy obtained her B.Tech in Electrical Engineering from Government Engineering College, Thrissur. She later pursued her M.Tech in Guided Missile from the Institute of Armament Technology (now Defence Institute of Advanced Technology). Her academic excellence laid the foundation for her remarkable career in missile technology.

1988 - 1998

Joining DRDO & Early Career

After completing her education, Tessy joined the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) in 1988. She started working on the Agni missile program under the guidance of Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam, who became her mentor. During these formative years, she developed expertise in solid propellant systems and inertial navigation.

1998 - 2009

Rising Through the Ranks

Tessy earned her doctorate while working at DRDO. She made significant contributions to various missile projects, particularly in the guidance systems of the Agni missile. Her technical acumen and leadership abilities were recognized, leading to increased responsibilities. Despite being in a male-dominated field, she continued to break barriers and excel in her work.

2009 - 2012

Making History

Dr. Thomas made history by becoming the Project Director for Agni-IV missile. This made her the first woman to lead a missile project in India. Under her leadership, the Agni-IV missile was successfully tested in 2011, marking a significant achievement in India's missile technology. Her work earned her the nickname "Missile Woman of India."

2012 - 2018

Leading Agni-V

Dr. Thomas became the Project Director (Mission) for Agni-V, India's first intercontinental ballistic missile. Under her leadership, Agni-V was successfully tested multiple times, significantly enhancing India's strategic defense capabilities. This cemented her reputation as one of India's most accomplished scientists and defense technologists.

2018 - Present

Continued Excellence & Inspiration

Dr. Thomas continued to rise in her career, taking on more senior roles within DRDO. She has been recognized with numerous awards and honors for her contributions to India's defense technology. Beyond her technical work, she has become a powerful inspirational figure for women in STEM fields, frequently giving talks and mentoring young scientists.
